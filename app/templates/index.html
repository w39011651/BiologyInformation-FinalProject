Introduction

This project aims to address the inefficiencies and high costs associated with experimentally identifying ATP binding sites in protein sequences. We developed a web-based prediction platform that leverages deep learning techniques to automatically identify potential ATP binding sites from raw protein sequences. This tool supports research in structural biology and drug discovery by improving early-stage target screening efficiency and reducing laboratory workload.

Approach

Our approach integrates advanced deep learning models to enhance prediction accuracy. Specifically, we employ Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN) for spatial feature extraction, the Evolutionary Scale Modeling (ESM) protein language model for capturing evolutionary context, and the Mamba architecture for efficient sequence modeling. These models are trained on curated datasets derived from publicly available protein structure and function databases. The development process includes data preprocessing, model training and validation, user interface prototyping, and multiple rounds of usability testing. The result is an intuitive and powerful tool for ATP binding site prediction.
